EventHive - Your Hub for Events at UQ

2024

University of Queensland | Product Designer

EventHive was designed as part of the DECO7250 Human-Computer Interaction course at The University of Queensland. Our team identified a major issue faced by students: the fragmented nature of campus event promotion and the hidden fees involved in ticketing. With over 220 clubs and societies, many students were unaware of most events happening around them, while organisers struggled to reach audiences effectively.

My role in this project included conducting user research, synthesising insights, and contributing to the design of the EventHive prototype. I worked closely with my team to analyse existing systems, develop personas and storyboards, and design a mobile-first solution that integrated event discovery, ticketing, and social interaction into one seamless platform.

THE CHALLENGE

Why was this problem worth solving?

Students consistently reported missing events because information was scattered across Instagram pages, posters, or word-of-mouth. Ticketing often relied on third-party platforms like Eventbrite, which added fees that inflated costs. This created frustration for students and reduced attendance at events that could have otherwise fostered community and belonging.

"I missed out on events just because I didn't hear about them until the last minute."

THE RESEARCH

How did we understand user needs?

We began with contextual inquiry, competitor analysis, and user research. We compared existing systems like UQU What's On, UQ Events, Eventbrite, and Humanitix. While each had strengths, none provided a complete solution for UQ students. Limitations included outdated UI, limited filtering options, and ticketing fees.

We then conducted short interviews with ~20 students and surveys with 9 students. Key findings included:

50% of students didn't know the UQU "What's On" calendar existed.

Social media was the primary way students heard about events.

Students valued event categories, personal calendars, and in-app ticket purchasing.

Motivation to attend increased if friends were going or if the process was easier.

DEFINING THE PROBLEM

How might we centralise event discovery and ticketing at UQ, while making it fair, transparent, and socially engaging for students?

IDEATION

How did we approach the solution?

We brainstormed solutions ranging from purely informational websites to social-first apps. Through feedback and iteration, we refined the idea into a mobile-first event platform that combined:

Discovery: Search, filters, recommendations, and categories.

Transaction: Integrated payments, autofill, and transparent ticketing.

Community: Club profiles, chats, and group features.

Our personas (e.g., Ye, a first-year student; Lisa, a Master’s student) helped us ground the solution in diverse user needs. Storyboards illustrated how EventHive would fit into a student's day, from finding an event to attending it with friends.

EventHive

EventHive is a holistic event ecosystem that combines discovery, booking, and social engagement into one seamless app. By centralising event information, removing ticketing fees, and adding community-driven features like chats and club profiles, EventHive transforms attending events from a scattered, frustrating process into an accessible, inclusive, and engaging campus experience.

The key features of the app are:

HOME PAGE

Welcome and Discovery Hub

The Home page serves as the central hub where students can discover upcoming events, see personalized recommendations, and quickly access their saved events. It features a clean, intuitive interface with event cards, search functionality, and quick access to popular clubs and trending events on campus.

Linked User Need:
Students said they often missed events because information was scattered across Instagram and posters.

FIND EVENTS & CLUBS

Comprehensive Discovery Experience

Students expressed frustration with unclear or incomplete event information. The Search and Event Details pages solve this by providing filters, transparent pricing, organiser details, and visual galleries. With everything accessible in one place, students can make informed decisions, discover new clubs, and confidently plan their participation in campus events.

Linked User Need:
Students wanted clearer event details and transparency, including who was hosting, pricing, and location.

EVENTS CALENDAR

Personal Event Management

The Events Calendar provides students with a personalized view of their saved events, upcoming activities, and schedule management. It integrates with their personal calendar, sends reminders, and allows them to track their event history and preferences for better future recommendations.

Linked User Need:
Students struggled to keep track of multiple events across different platforms.

TICKET PURCHASING

Seamless Booking Experience

The Ticket Purchasing feature eliminates traditional ticketing fees and provides a streamlined booking process. Students can purchase tickets directly through the app with secure payment options, receive instant confirmations, and manage their ticket collection all in one place.

Linked User Need:
Students disliked hidden service charges and long, multi-step checkouts on Eventbrite.

"I hate filling in my student details all the time I try to buy tickets on different platforms."

CHAT & COMMUNITY

Social Engagement Platform

The Chat and Community feature fosters social connections by allowing students to join event-specific group chats, share experiences, and build relationships with fellow attendees. It includes club discussion forums, event updates, and a platform for students to connect before, during, and after events.

Linked User Need:
Students wanted to attend events with friends and connect with like-minded peers.

USER TESTING

Did the solution meet user expectations?

We built a high-fidelity prototype in Figma and tested it with students to evaluate usability, satisfaction, and task efficiency. Our evaluation combined heuristic evaluations, think-aloud tests, and task-based studies

Key tasks included:

  • • Finding an event using the search function.
  • • Purchasing a ticket with autofill enabled.
  • • Accessing a purchased ticket QR code.
  • • Joining a club and exploring its events.
Metric Result
Success rate All participants were able to complete the core tasks successfully.
Time-on-task On average, students completed ticket purchases in under 1 minute, significantly faster than Eventbrite's multi-step checkout.
Satisfaction Most students reported that the app felt "clear," "fast," and "less stressful" compared to existing systems.
Navigation The persistent bottom navigation bar helped users quickly return to the home screen or tickets page.
Pain points Some students wanted a clearer distinction between personal chats and group event chats. Others suggested adding push notifications for ticket reminders.
SUS Score Average SUS (System Usability Scale) score: 84, indicating excellent usability.
User Preference 90% of participants said they would prefer EventHive over current ticketing methods.
Event Attendance 80% of participants said the simplified ticketing flow would make them attend more events.
ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

What risks did we need to address while designing?

We value data privacy and align to the ACS (Australian Computer Society) Code of Ethics in our design approach. Understanding that user data is sensitive, we implemented several safeguards to protect user privacy and maintain ethical standards.

Key ethical considerations include:

  • • Data collection for user understanding is completely optional
  • • Users have full authority to disable data collection at any time
  • • Transparent privacy policies and clear consent mechanisms
  • • Ensuring the app benefits both students and clubs equally.
  • • Preventing ticket scalping or bulk resales.
  • • Accessibility (contrast levels, readability, multi-language support).
REFLECTION

What did I learn from this project?

This project highlighted the importance of grounding design in real user frustrations. Our research revealed that awareness and affordability were just as critical as usability. I learned how to balance the needs of students, clubs, and administrators while still delivering a clean, student-friendly experience. I also developed skills in competitor analysis, user research synthesis, and iterative prototyping.

Looking ahead, EventHive could integrate features like hashtags, UQ Maps, premium memberships, and organiser tools to scale its impact.
